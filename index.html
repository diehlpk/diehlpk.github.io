--- layout: page title: Welcome! excerpt: "Patrick Diehl's personal website" search_omit: true --- My name is Patrick Diehl, a research scientist at the Center for Computation and Technology and adjunct faculty at the Department of Physics & Astronomy at the Louisiana State University. Before joining LSU, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the Laboratory for Multiscale Mechanics at Polytechnique Montreal. I received my diploma in computer science at the University of Stuttgart and my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from the University of Bonn. My main research interests are:
  1. Computational engineering with the focus on peridynamic material models for the application in solids, like glassy or composite materials
  2. Developing new numerical methods for non-local models, multi-scale methods, and coupling non-local and local models.
  3. High performance computing, especially the asynchronous many task system (ATM), e.g. the C++ standard library for parallelism and concurrency (HPX) for large heterogeneous computations.
In addition, I have a deep interest in the usage of Open source software to enhance the openness of Science. With respect to teaching, I am interested to develop tools to easily introduce C++ and parallel computing to non-computer science students. I am a topic editor for the Journal of Open Source Software (JOSS). I am the vice-chair of the USNCM technical thrust area for Large Scale Structural Systems and Optimal Design.
