--- layout: page title: Welcome! search_omit: true --- My name is Patrick Diehl. I am currently a research scientist in Applied Computer Science at Los Alamos National Laboratory and also serve as adjunct faculty in the Department of Physics & Astronomy at Louisiana State University. Previously, I held a position as a research scientist at the Center for Computation and Technology at LSU. Prior to my time at LSU, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the Laboratory for Multiscale Mechanics at Polytechnique Montreal. I earned my diploma in computer science from the University of Stuttgart and completed my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics at the University of Bonn. My primary research interests include:
  1. Computational engineering with the focus on peridynamic material models for the application in solids, like glassy or composite materials
  2. Developing new numerical methods for non-local models, multi-scale methods, and coupling non-local and local models.
  3. High performance computing, especially the asynchronous many task system (ATM), e.g. the C++ standard library for parallelism and concurrency (HPX) for large heterogeneous computations.
In addition, I have a deep interest in the usage of Open source software to enhance the openness of Science. With respect to teaching, I am interested to develop tools to easily introduce C++ and parallel computing to non-computer science students. I am a topic editor for the Journal of Open Source Software (JOSS). I am the vice-chair of the USNCM technical thrust area for Large Scale Structural Systems and Optimal Design. I am a co-author of the book "Parallel C++: Efficient and Scalable High-Performance Parallel Programming Using HPX".
